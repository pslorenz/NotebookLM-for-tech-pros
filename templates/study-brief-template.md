# Study Brief Template

Fill this in before opening NotebookLM or Claude. If you cannot fill it in, you have not scoped the topic well enough to study it productively yet.

Save one of these per study session. Over time, the collection becomes its own artifact.

---

## Brief

**Date:**

**Topic:**

Scope it narrow enough that you could teach a peer the topic in forty-five minutes. Broad topics produce shallow notebooks. If you find yourself writing "everything about X," keep narrowing.

**Operational outcome:**

What exists at the end of this session that did not exist at the beginning? A passed exam item, a deployed control, a written policy, a briefing, a detection rule, a decision made. The outcome shapes every subsequent choice. If the outcome is "understand the topic better," rewrite it. That is not an outcome, it is a wish.

**Current level:**

Honest self-assessment. Never touched it, conceptually familiar, deployed once, deployed regularly, teaching it to others. Overstating your level produces sourcing that skips foundational material you actually needed.

**Time budget:**

In minutes or hours, for this specific session. Defended on the calendar. Do not plan a session you will not actually sit through.

**Sources (initial):**

List the three to five sources you already know you want to include. Weighted primary. The rest of the sourcing happens during the session itself.

## Post-session self-assessment

One paragraph of prose, honest. What do you know now that you did not know before? What do you still not understand? What would you do differently next session? This becomes the input for the next brief on the same or adjacent topic.
